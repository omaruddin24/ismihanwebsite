Who I Am

Medicine as a calling,
not just a career.

I'm a third-year medical student at the Chicago College of Osteopathic Medicine (anticipated graduation May 2027), where I'm training to become a physician-scientist committed to health equity, community care, and translational research.

My path into medicine was shaped by an early recognition that the most pressing health challenges aren't only biological Ś they're social, structural, and deeply human. That realization led me to found Chi-Care Mobile Medical Services, a student-run organization providing free primary care and health navigation to Chicago's unhoused populations.

Before medical school, I graduated Summa Cum Laude from the University of Illinois at Chicago with a B.S. in Biochemistry and a minor in Sociology Ś a pairing that captures my dual commitment to scientific depth and social context.

Outside the clinic and lab, I serve as a Schweitzer Fellow, a TA for physiology and medical coursework, a humanitarian volunteer in Bosnia and Herzegovina, and an advocate for osteopathic medicine at the national level. My work is guided by a belief that healing requires presence Ś in the community, in the policy room, and at the bedside.
